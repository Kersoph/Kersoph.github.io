A colony building strategy game with a unique depth

In a deeply simulated world, every action has a reaction. Every animal and every plant tries to survive while you take care of your villagers. Manage your citizens, resources, production lines and the interaction with the complex environment. How much will you intervene in the course of nature? You will decide.

Build Your Economy

Manage the acquisition, refinement and distribution of resources. The goods must be transported to the desired location by someone actually having the time and space to do it.

Care About Your Villagers

Manage the needs of the citizens. They are “alive” and have a specific home, workplace, and unique needs. Also animals are looking for food, get thirsty, sleepy and form groups to find their place in nature. Even plants need water and try to survive.

Find Balance (Or Not)

Humans share the same space and resources with nature and influence each other. While in the beginning the influence of the humans is insignificant due to the small size, it gets more and more important when the city and its needs grow. Risk of extinction and risk of overpopulation may form depending on the actions of the player.

Observe The Vibrant World

The Seasons and the weather affects the gameplay. Certain plants only grow in certain seasons and the temperature and wheater affects the Humans, Animals, Plants, and the Environment.

Take Your Time

You set the pace. Pause the game, speed it up or slow it down whenever you want. Nature is robust and your villagers unlock more complex needs with your progression.

Build Your Own Scenarios

Create own maps and scenarios in the editor. The whole landscape can be changed and the objects on it. Add new maps or link existing ones together to a new world. You can connect water flows and create paths for travelers.

Additional Information

The planned target platforms are desktop Windows, Linux and MacOS with translations in English and German. More support might be added later.

The Circle of Kerzoven is developed by Marco Burri with the Godot Engine. He started developing games over ten years ago besides his career as an automation technician and later software engineer. One of his most famous game is Legend of Ahssûn, where he was working in the core team as a generalist and is still supporting the project today.
